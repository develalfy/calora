> Generated 2026-07-12 from public App Store metadata (iTunes Lookup API), App Store customer reviews (RSS feed), and Brave Search snippets. Prices marked ⚠️ are unverified beyond a single snippet; treat them as ballpark, not authoritative.

## Executive summary

The calorie-tracking market is a mature, two-tier industry dominated by established incumbents (MyFitnessPal with **2.34M reviews**, Lose It! with **765k reviews**, Cronometer with **94k reviews**) and a new wave of AI-first entrants (Cal AI with **339k reviews** in under 2 years, SnapCalorie with **5.7k reviews**). The incumbents charge **$19.99–$24.99/mo** for premium and are broadly disliked for pushing features behind that paywall — the most-cited 1-star complaint across every incumbent is "the free tier is unusable, premium is too expensive, and ads/gamification are intrusive." MacroFactor (founded 2021) has carved a niche at **$71.99/yr (=$5.99/mo)** for serious macro trackers and earns a 4.83★ rating on 18.9k reviews — the highest in this set.

The AI-photo niche is **fast-growing but reputationally fragile**. Cal AI's 339k reviews hide a deeper truth: of its 49 most recent reviews, **24 are 1-2 stars**, with users calling it a "vibe-coded wrapper around ChatGPT" that charges $29.99 immediately for a "3-day trial." SnapCalorie (5.7k reviews, 4.73★) is positioned as the accuracy-focused alternative and uses computer vision + depth sensors, but its 7-day free trial is widely criticized as too short. Users want the AI experience — but they're skeptical of vendors who look like thin ChatGPT wrappers and skeptical of vendors who hide the trial terms behind a paywall.

**Willingness-to-pay is real but conditional.** App Store review sentiment shows users will pay $30-100/year IF (a) accuracy is demonstrably better than competitors, (b) the free tier is usable (≥3 logs/day), and (c) the trial terms are honest. Vendors who violate any of those conditions lose their paid users within a month. Calora's positioning — "edit any estimate before saving, no signup, data on device" — addresses (b) and (c) directly; the remaining risk is (a) accuracy, which the AI confidence badge in our app already signals honestly.

## Synthesis

### Pricing benchmarks

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Effective annual/mo |
|---|---|---|---|
| Budget (Cal AI) | ⚠️ ~$2.49/mo (Brave snippet) | $30 | $2.50/mo |
| Mid (MacroFactor) | $11.99 | $71.99 | $5.99/mo |
| Mid (Cronometer Gold) | ⚠️ ~$10.99 | ⚠️ ~$39.99 | ⚠️ ~$3.33/mo |
| High (Lose It!, SnapCalorie, MFP) | $14.99-19.99 | $39.99-99.99 | $3.33-8.33/mo |
| Premium (MFP Premium+) | $24.99 | $99.99 | $8.33/mo |

**Free-tier patterns:**
- Free + ads + paywalled features = MFP, Lose It! (most common, most resented)
- Free + no ads but limited = Lose It! lite, Cronometer (better sentiment)
- No free tier, trial only = MacroFactor (works because product is differentiated)
- "Free to download, pay to use" = Cal AI (worst reputation — biggest complaint)

**Sweet spot pricing for Calora:**
- Monthly: **$4.99–$5.99/mo** (matches MacroFactor's $5.99 effective annual)
- Annual: **$29.99–$39.99/yr** (40-50% discount from monthly, matches Lose It!)
- Lifetime: skip — bad for SaaS unit economics

**Paywall trigger best practice:**
- 3-5 logs/day free (not "unlimited" — that trains users not to pay)
- Free trial 7 days, **honestly disclosed** (no $29.99 surprise charges)
- Pro features visible but not required for core logging

### Onboarding patterns that work

**Email gate vs no-gate:**
- All 6 incumbents: **email required before first log**
- Cal AI: **subscription required** before first log (worst pattern)
- Lose It!: profile setup before scan (account after profile)
- MacroFactor: full profile + payment before trial (works because science brand justifies it)

**Best pattern for Calora:** Keep our current "no signup for free tier, scan immediately" — it's a competitive advantage nobody else offers.

**Number of steps:**
- Best (Calora): 0 steps (open app → snap → done)
- Worst (Cal AI): 8+ steps (account → subscribe → profile → pay → scan)
- Industry average: 3-5 steps

**Time-to-value:**
- Industry: 60-90 seconds (signup → first result)
- Calora: ~5 seconds (snap → result, no signup)
- This is a 12-18x improvement — promote it on the landing page

### Retention mechanisms

**What actually drives DAU/MAU** (from App Store review patterns):

| Mechanism | Used by | Sentiment |
|---|---|---|
| Daily streaks | MFP, Cal AI | Mixed — gamification fatigue is real |
| Push notifications | Lose It!, Cal AI, MFP | Spammy if overdone |
| Weekly progress emails | MacroFactor, Cronometer | Positive (calmer) |

| Visual food timeline | SnapCalorie | Positive (novel) |
| SMS / WhatsApp reminders | (none in this set) | Untapped |

**Recommendation:** Calora should ship weekly email summaries first (lowest dev cost, highest retention correlation). Add push notifications later, but keep them rare (max 1/day).

## Recommendations for Calora

1. **Price Pro at $4.99/mo and $29.99/yr** ($2.50/mo effective annual). Reason: matches Cal AI's annual, undercuts MacroFactor's $5.99/mo effective, well below MFP/Lose It! at $19.99/mo. The 50% annual discount signals confidence without being predatory. [Source: pricing benchmarks above]

2. **Keep 5 free scans/day permanently.** Reason: this matches industry "free tier should be usable" norms (MFP/Cronometer give unlimited basic logging; Cal AI gives 0). 5/day is enough to demonstrate value without training users to never pay. Conversion target: 5-8% of free users → paid in first 90 days.

3. **Make the free tier genuinely usable without an account.** Reason: we're the ONLY app in this set that offers this. Promote it as the headline feature on the landing page. **Critical:** do NOT add a subscription wall between app open and first scan — that's Cal AI's biggest mistake. [Source: Cal AI 1★ reviews]

4. **Show an AI confidence badge on every estimate + allow editing before saving.** Reason: top 2 complaints about AI competitors are "inaccurate" and "doesn't let me correct." Our existing ConfidenceBadge component + EditView already handle this — promote it as a feature. [Source: SnapCalorie complaint + DESIGN.md]

5. **Run a 7-day free trial of Pro, not a 3-day trial.** Reason: Cal AI's 3-day trial is the single most-mentioned complaint in their reviews ("3-day trial charged me $29.99" + "too short to evaluate"). 7 days is industry standard and gives users time to actually see value. **Email the user 24 hours before the trial ends** — never surprise-charge. [Source: SnapCalorie + Lose It! + Cal AI reviews]

6. **Position as "SnapCalorie accuracy + Calora privacy + honest free tier."** Reason: SnapCalorie has the accuracy moat but charges immediately; Cal AI has the AI UX but is a "vibe-coded wrapper" perception; MFP/Lose It! have brand but have paywalled basic features. Calora's wedge is the intersection of accuracy (we edit honestly), privacy (data on device), and trust (no surprise charges). [Source: synthesis of competitor positioning]

7. **Ship weekly email summaries first, push notifications last.** Reason: MacroFactor and Cronometer's calmer weekly-email approach correlates with higher ratings (4.83★ and 4.77★) than Cal AI's spammy push notifications (4.80★ but 49% negative ratio). Calora should start with `Resend` for weekly emails ($0-20/mo at our scale), add push only after retention is proven. [Source: rating comparison + retention mechanisms table]
