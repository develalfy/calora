# Competitive Landscape — Calorie Trackers

## Direct competitors

### MyFitnessPal (50M+ users, owned by Francisco Partners)
- **Strengths**: Massive food database, barcode scanning, recipe importer, social
- **Weaknesses**: Cluttered UI, food logging takes 30-60s, manual search dominant, ads + premium upsell
- **AI features**: Recently added "Meal Scan" but it's bolted-on, still requires signup, still slow
- **Why we win**: Photo-first, zero signup, <30s flow

### Cronometer (premium niche)
- **Strengths**: Best micronutrient accuracy, loved by biohackers
- **Weaknesses**: Database-heavy UX, intimidating for casual users
- **AI features**: None
- **Why we win**: Casual users don't need 80 nutrients; they want fast logging

### Lose It! (Snap It feature)
- **Strengths**: Has photo recognition since 2017, decent
- **Weaknesses**: Often wrong, forces manual correction, requires account
- **AI features**: "Snap It" uses image recognition but pre-LLM, lower accuracy
- **Why we win**: GPT-4o/Claude vision is 2 generations ahead of their CV pipeline

### AI-native entrants
- **Cal AI** ($5/mo, 5M+ downloads) — closest to our thesis
- **MacroFactor** — science-focused, no photo
- **Arise** — text-based AI coach, not photo-first

## Cal AI analysis (the one to beat)
- Photo → estimate works, claims 90% accuracy
- Subscription $5/mo or $30/yr
- Good UX, mobile-first
- **Their gap**: No real edit-before-save (you accept or reject the whole thing)
- **Our edge**: Edit individual items/portions, text fallback for "I didn't take a photo"

## Positioning
**"Snap your meal, get calories in 10 seconds. No signup, no barcode, no database hunting."**

Target the person who downloaded MyFitnessPal, used it for 3 days, then deleted it because logging felt like homework.

## Differentiation that matters
1. **Zero signup** — biggest friction removed
2. **Edit individual items** — vs all-or-nothing accept
3. **Text fallback** — when you forget to snap
4. **PWA, not native** — no App Store gate, instant install
