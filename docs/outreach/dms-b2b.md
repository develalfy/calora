## Universal personalization rule

Before sending any of these, do **exactly one** of:
1. **Look at the company's actual app/site** — find one specific thing to reference (their meal log UX, a recent feature they shipped, a public API they shipped). Use that in the subject + first line.
2. **Look at their public engineering blog or LinkedIn** — name a specific person, ideally the founder/CTO.
3. **If you can't find either:** skip. Don't send a generic DM.

A specific first line > a generic polished pitch.

## Pattern: "See + Sequence"

Each DM follows this structure (works in 30-second mobile reads):

1. **Reference** — one specific thing about *their* product (shows you did homework)
2. **Question** — frames their pain, hints you have an answer
3. **Bridge** — what I built (one sentence + link)
4. **Ask** — low-friction (15-min demo, 6-line embed, etc.)

No screenshots. No feature lists. No pricing tables in the DM itself — pricing lives at /b2b for the prospect to read if they want.
